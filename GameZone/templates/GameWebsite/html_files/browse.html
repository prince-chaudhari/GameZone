Tic Tac Toe

Tic Tac Toe, also known as noughts and crosses, is a simple yet engaging game played on a 3x3 grid. Two players take turns marking empty squares with either an 'X' or an 'O', aiming to be the first to complete a row, column, or diagonal with their symbol. The player who succeeds in placing three of their marks in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal row wins the game. If all nine squares are filled without either player achieving a winning combination, the game ends in a draw.

Rock Paper Scissors

Rock Paper Scissors is a simple and fun hand game played between two people. Each player simultaneously forms one of three shapes with their hand: a closed fist representing 'rock', an open hand representing 'paper', or a hand with two fingers extended representing 'scissors'. The rules are straightforward: rock crushes scissors, scissors cuts paper, and paper covers rock. The objective is to outsmart your opponent by predicting their next move while simultaneously choosing the winning gesture.

Hangman

Hangman is a classic word-guessing game where one player thinks of a word and the other player tries to guess it by suggesting letters. The word to be guessed is represented by a row of dashes, each dash representing a letter in the word. The guessing player suggests letters one at a time, trying to guess the word correctly before running out of attempts. If the suggested letter is present in the word, it is revealed in its correct position(s); if not, a part of a stick figure (the 'hangman') is drawn as a penalty.

Memory Card

The Memory Card Game is a popular cognitive matching game where players test their memory skills by uncovering pairs of matching cards. The game begins with a set of cards arranged face-down in a grid formation. Players take turns flipping over two cards at a time, trying to find matching pairs. If a player successfully matches two cards, they remove them from play and earn points. However, if the cards do not match, they are flipped back face-down, and the next player takes their turn.

Ping Pong Game

Ping Pong, also known as table tennis, is a fast-paced and exciting racket sport played on a table divided by a net. Players use small paddles to hit a lightweight ball back and forth across the table, aiming to score points by making the ball bounce on the opponent's side without them being able to return it. The game can be played as singles (one player on each side) or doubles (two players on each side). Players must utilize quick reflexes, hand-eye coordination, and strategic positioning to outmaneuver their opponent and win points. The first player or team to reach a predetermined number of points wins the game.
